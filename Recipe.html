How to make chicken soup

Here's a classic recipe for Chicken Soup:

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (about 3-4 pounds), cleaned and cut into parts.
  • 1 onion, peeled and halved.
  • 2 carrots, peeled and chopped.
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped.
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced.
  • 8 cups water.
  • 1 bay leaf.
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme.
  • Salt and pepper to taste.
  • 1 cup egg noodles or rice (optional).
  • Fresh parsley for garnish (optional).

Instructions

1. Prepare the Chicken: - Rinse the chicken parts under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels.

2. Create the Broth: - In a large soup pot, combine the chicken parts, halved onion, chopped carrots, celery, minced garlic, bay leaf, dried thyme, and water.

3. Simmer: - Place the pot over medium-high heat and bring it to a boil. - Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 1 to 1.5 hours. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface.

4. Remove Chicken and Strain: - Carefully remove the chicken pieces from the pot and set them aside to cool. - Strain the broth to remove the solids, leaving you with a clear chicken broth.

5. Shred the Chicken: - Once the chicken has cooled, remove the meat from the bones and shred it into bite-sized pieces.

6. Return Chicken to the Pot: - Add the shredded chicken back to the pot with the clear broth.

7. Season and Add Noodles/Rice: - Season the soup with salt and pepper to taste. - If you like, you can add egg noodles or rice to the soup. Cook until the noodles or rice are tender, following the package instructions.

8. Serve: - Ladle the chicken soup into bowls. - Garnish with fresh parsley if desired. - Serve hot, and enjoy!
